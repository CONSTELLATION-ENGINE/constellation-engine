🧠 What is curiosity-driven autonomy? click to expand

Most assistants wait for you to speak. Mímir wakes itself — but only when its memory has something to say.

While you are away, every concept in Mímir's graph carries a real activation level that decays over time. Touch a topic and it lights up; ignore it and it fades. Curiosity is what happens when one zone refuses to fade — when something you said hours ago keeps lighting up its neighbors faster than it loses charge.

This is not retrieval-on-demand or a cron job wearing a personality. It is a continuous activation field, running between your conversations, letting interest accumulate or die on its own terms.

Five gates · all must align
  • 01≈ 2.5 min of silence in the activation field (300 idle ticks)
  • 02≥ 10 min since the last wakeup (cooldown)
  • 03No wakeup currently in flight (single-flight, hard rule)
  • 04< 5 wakeups within the last 30 min (safety cap)
  • 05Strongest semantic zone's mean activation > 0.008
How it actually moves

The decay race. If the same theme is touched again before fade-out, it accumulates — eventually crossing the trigger floor. Topics nobody revisits forget themselves. Quieter days produce a quieter Mímir; busy days surface the busiest threads.

Why not a scheduled job? A schedule fires on time, not on signal. It speaks even when there is nothing worth saying.

Why not random? Randomness cannot point to what it cares about. Every Mímir wakeup names a specific zone and top node — you can trace the exact thread that crossed the floor.

What we mean by stateful cognition Not a model pretending to remember between turns, but a substrate where memory has weight, attention has cost, and curiosity is a measurable threshold being crossed in real time. Try it: trigger a wakeup, then ignore that topic for an hour and watch the same zone fade. The mechanism is yours to play with.
